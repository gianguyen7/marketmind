Finding

Polymarket is efficient. We could not beat it.

We asked whether binary prediction markets on Polymarket carry structural inefficiencies that an ML model can exploit. Across four independent attempts (a structural rule, calibration correction, trajectory dynamics, and a global ML regressor), none produced a material improvement over the market price alone on held-out test markets.

Where the market is wrong
Government policy under-prices longshots
A +17.4pp longshot bias on government-policy markets. Low-probability events resolve more often than prices imply. Too small-n (189) to trade reliably.
Long horizons drift
Calibration degrades ~5× from <1d (Brier 0.013) to 3 to 12 months (0.094). >1y markets show meaningful miscalibration, ECE 0.101.
Sports is the gold standard
Best-calibrated large category: Brier 0.0650 across 2,099 markets. Deep liquidity + short horizons + frequent resolution discipline the crowd.
Bottom line

The crowd wins, but we now know where and why.

Polymarket's crowd aggregates information well and calibrates probabilities accurately (reliability 0.0004). Residual miscalibration lives in under-sampled categories (government policy, geopolitics) and at very long horizons, pockets too small or too fragile to trade systematically. The most valuable output of this project is the characterization of where and how the market works, not a model that beats it.
