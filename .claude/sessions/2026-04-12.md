### Decisions
- **Regime-invariance discipline adopted as the primary research framing.** The earlier "find where the market is wrong and exploit it locally" was vulnerable to the devil's advocate critique that financial time series are non-stationary. Reframed to: produce structural, falsifiable hypotheses about *mechanisms* (not themes), and validate with walk-forward on post-freeze resolutions. See `docs/research_plan.md` new §Regime-Invariance Discipline. This supersedes the 2026-04-11 session 1 framing.
- **A2 mechanism REJECTED by pre-registered criteria.** Two falsification triggers fired: sign flip on `pct_lifetime_elapsed` and `log_volume` between train/val, plus secondary `price_vs_open` dominating all primaries. Root cause: train has only 2 markets — any feature is essentially market identity. Honest read: the split is underpowered, not the mechanism is wrong. Track A stops at A1; the mechanism defers to Track B5.
- **Track B1 scope trimmed after discovering existing code.** Original plan had 8 sub-steps; real gaps were only B1b (config), B1e (event grouping), B1f (CLI flags). B1c (categories.yaml), B1d (resolve_outcomes logging), and smoke tests deferred as unnecessary.
- **A2 population threshold changed from absolute ($100k) to empirical (bottom-25% volume).** Dry-run showed min volume at the $1M filter was $53M, making absolute dollar thresholds meaningless. Now defined as bottom quartile of fetched universe intersected with max days_to_end > 90.
- **`--gamma-fetch` does NOT re-run curated pipeline.** Caught mid-launch that the original code would have overwritten `data/processed/` which is frozen until Track B3. Patched to stop after Gamma step.

## Session 17:00 — B3 split, B5 walk-forward, calibration analysis, C1 recalibration, C4 trajectory dynamics

### Context
User resumed to execute the full pipeline from B3 (expanded data split) through to exploitation attempts. Session completed B3, B5, calibration analysis, and two model-building tracks (C1 calibration correction, C4 trajectory dynamics), culminating in a definitive finding: Polymarket is efficient and leaves very little room for systematic improvement.

### New information
- Category classifier improvement: "other" dropped from 862 (17%) to 253 (5%) by adding `social_media` category and expanding sports/politics/crypto/entertainment/science/economy patterns.
- B3 expanded dataset: 4,538 markets (after filtering 373 sparse <5 snapshots), 639,355 snapshots, 11 categories. Train/val/test = 2,619/899/1,020 markets.
- B5 structural population: 106/52/61 markets in train/val/test (bottom-Q25 volume AND max days_to_end > 90).
- `log_volume` signal fails within structural pop due to range restriction: volume range $1.3M–$1.7M (std $113K) — no meaningful variation. Works on full population.
- YES under-pricing is real: +0.48 mean error on YES-resolved structural-pop markets. But only 19-25% of structural-pop markets resolve YES.
- Polymarket overall calibration: Brier 0.079, reliability 0.0004, ECE 1.1% across 4,538 markets. Remarkably well-calibrated.
- Classic F-L bias confirmed: longshots (price < 0.3) win 2.2pp more than implied, favourites (price > 0.7) win 3.9pp less. Government_policy has massive +17.4pp longshot bias.
- Category × horizon worst pockets: geopolitics >1y (Brier 0.574), science_tech >1y (0.422), government_policy 3m-1y (0.337).
- Staleness (snapshots since last price move) correlates ρ=-0.743 with |market_error| — stale markets are confident and correct, not stuck at wrong prices. Inverts the intuitive hypothesis.
- Platt scaling improves test Brier by 0.0001 (0.1%). Per-category and category×horizon models make things worse (overfitting).
- Trajectory hybrid (45% XGB + 55% market price) improves test Brier by 0.0006 (0.7%). Standalone XGB is worse than naive.

### Decisions
- **B3 completed with per-category temporal split.** 60/20/20 within each of 11 categories. Cross-category event groups resolved by taking most conservative (earliest) split. All leakage checks pass. Old curated splits backed up as `.curated_backup`.
- **B5 structural hypothesis: NOT CONFIRMED.** 2/3 primary signals confirmed (days_to_end +, pct_lifetime_elapsed -), but log_volume flips on val (range restriction). Frozen alpha-shift rule loses money on every split (-$464/61 test bets). Identification problem unsolved.
- **Pivoted from "exploit mispricing" to "calibration characterization."** The structural hypothesis describes a real phenomenon but is not actionable. F-L bias is real but too small to exploit. Research value is in characterization, not trading rules.
- **C1 calibration correction: near-zero improvement.** Market reliability 0.0004 is already near-optimal. More granular models overfit. Closed this line.
- **C4 trajectory dynamics: small improvement (0.7%).** Hybrid model helps but standalone is worse than naive. Not enough to justify a correction model. Fourth and final exploitation attempt.
- **Polymarket is efficient.** Definitive conclusion across four independent approaches. Project pivots fully to C2 (dashboard) and C3 (research write-up).
- **Subagent Write permissions fixed.** Added Read/Write/Edit/Glob/Grep to `.claude/settings.local.json`.
- **Research backlog established.** Four ideas for future exploration: event group dynamics, volume-price divergence, resolution surprise prediction, convergence speed prediction.
